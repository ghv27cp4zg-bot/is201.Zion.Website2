Why Zion?

Nestled in the southwest corner of Utah, Zion National Park is one of the most incredible landscapes I have ever seen. Towering sandstone cliffs, slot canyons carved by the Virgin River, and trails that range from easy walks to heart-pounding ridge scrambles — Zion has something for everyone.

I first visited Zion and immediately fell in love with it. The combination of physical challenge, stunning scenery, and the quiet you only find deep in a canyon makes it my favorite place to hike every year.

My Top Zion Trails

Here are my favorite hikes ranked from hardest to easiest, with details about each one:

  1. Angels Landing (5.4 miles - Hard)
    • Elevation gain: 1,488 feet
    • Famous for the chain-assisted scramble at the top
    • Requires a permit — apply through recreation.gov
    • Best time to go: early morning before it gets crowded
  2. The Narrows — Top Down (16 miles - Strenuous)
    • You hike inside the Virgin River the whole way
    • Requires a permit and shuttle reservation
    • Rent a drysuit and neoprene socks — the water is freezing
    • One of the most unique hikes in the entire world
  3. Observation Point (8 miles - Hard)
    • Elevation gain: 2,148 feet
    • The view from the top looks DOWN onto Angels Landing
    • Way fewer crowds than Angels Landing
  4. Canyon Overlook Trail (1 mile - Easy)
    • Perfect for beginners or families
    • Short but has amazing views of the canyon entrance
    • No permit needed
  5. Emerald Pools (1.2 to 3 miles - Easy to Moderate)
    • Three pools connected by a trail system
    • Waterfalls in the spring are incredible
    • Great option right when you arrive at the park

Hiking Tips

Good preparation makes all the difference. Here is what I have learned from hiking Zion:

Stay Hydrated

Bring at least 3 liters of water per person on hot days. Dehydration sneaks up fast in the desert heat.

Good Footwear

Wear waterproof hiking boots with ankle support. Sticky rubber soles help a lot on slickrock.

Start Early

Hit the trailhead by 6 or 7 AM in summer. Crowds and temperatures both spike by 10 AM.

Download Maps

Cell signal is spotty in the canyon. Download offline maps on AllTrails before you go.

Get Permits Early

Angels Landing requires a permit. Apply through recreation.gov — the lottery fills up months in advance.

Pack the Essentials

Navigation, headlamp, sun protection, first aid kit, food, water, and extra layers.
